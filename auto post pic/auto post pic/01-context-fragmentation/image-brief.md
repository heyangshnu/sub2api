# Image Brief: Context Fragmentation

## Target Audience
English-speaking developers, freelancers, and knowledge workers who use multiple AI tools daily

## Language
English

## Tone
Relatable, slightly frustrated, conversational

## Core Message
Your AI doesn't remember your work. You spend more time re-establishing context than actually using AI.

## Carousel Structure
5 slides — Problem → Evidence → Cost → Insight → Solution

## Slide-by-Slide

### Slide 1: Hook
**Headline:** "Your AI has amnesia."
**Subtext:** Every morning, you re-explain your project. Again.
**Visual:** Split screen: left side shows a person typing a long prompt, right side shows an empty chat window with "How can I help you today?"

### Slide 2: The Evidence
**Headline:** "47 conversations. Same project. Zero memory."
**Subtext:** Finding that architecture decision from February? Like finding a needle in a haystack.
**Visual:** Scattered chat bubbles with blurred text, representing 47 conversations about one project

### Slide 3: The Cost
**Headline:** "20-40 minutes. Every morning. Just to remind your AI who you are."
**Subtext:** That's 2+ hours per week lost to context re-establishment.
**Visual:** Clock graphic showing 20-40 minutes highlighted, with a weekly calendar showing the accumulation

### Slide 4: The Insight
**Headline:** "The bottleneck isn't model speed. It's context persistence."
**Subtext:** You don't need a faster AI. You need an AI that remembers.
**Visual:** Two paths diverging: "Faster AI" (dead end) vs "AI that remembers" (open road)

### Slide 5: The Solution
**Headline:** "Your AI assistant. In your pocket. Always ready."
**Subtext:** OpenClaw — plug in, and your AI picks up right where you left off.
**Visual:** A sleek USB drive with a soft glow, being plugged into a laptop. On screen, the same workspace, same files, same conversation — seamless continuation. The drive feels like a key that unlocks your personal AI.

## Words to Avoid
- "Revolutionary" (overused)
- "Game-changing" (cliché)
- "Disrupt" (corporate jargon)
- "Seamless" (meaningless)
- "AI-powered" (redundant in context)
- "Lobster" (brand name not used)
- "USB" (sounds generic — use "drive" or "device")

## Visual Copy Rules
- Keep headlines under 8 words
- Subtext under 15 words
- No emoji in image text
- Use high contrast for readability
- Dark background with light text for modern feel
- Brand name: OpenClaw (not Lobster USB)
